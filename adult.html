For Adults & Caregivers

This page explains the project concept, who it supports, and the safety/consent principles behind an AI voice chatbot for grief healing for children facing a parent’s serious illness or hospice care.

The problem we aim to solve

Children may experience intense grief before a parent passes away (anticipatory grief) and after loss. Families often lack accessible, child-centered tools to help kids talk about feelings, ask questions, and preserve meaningful memories.

  • Kids may feel confused, scared, sad, or guilty without the words to explain it.
  • Caregivers may struggle to start hard conversations or maintain emotional routines.
  • Traditional support can be expensive, limited, or short-term.
Focus: children • illness/hospice loss

At-a-glance

  • What: AI voice chatbot concept
  • Goal: grief processing + memory sharing
  • Users: children + caregivers
  • Sponsor: Dr. Young
Caregiver reflection prompt: “How would you feel if your child used a tool like this?”
This supports consent, boundaries, and family comfort.

How the chatbot helps (examples)

  • Before loss: guided prompts help kids name feelings and prepare for changes.
  • Memory building: children can save favorite stories and moments with their parent.
  • After loss: reflective check-ins and calming activities support coping.
Design intention: supportive conversation and structure — not replacing caregivers or therapy.

Ethics, safety, and consent

  • Consent-first: caregivers approve any saved content and voice training.
  • Transparency: the system clearly states it is AI, not the real person.
  • Boundaries: avoids medical advice and crisis handling; redirects to trusted adults/pros.
  • Privacy: minimize data collection; protect children’s information.
This is an academic concept landing page and does not provide medical or therapeutic services. If someone feels unsafe or in crisis, contact local emergency services or a trusted professional.
