Purpose

The problem we’re solving

Children experiencing a parent’s serious illness (including hospice care) often face anticipatory grief before death occurs. Families may not have accessible, affordable, developmentally appropriate tools to help children express feelings, ask questions, and preserve meaningful memories.

  • Kids may feel confusion, fear, sadness, or guilt without the words to explain it.
  • Parents/caregivers may not know how to start grief conversations or support coping.
  • Support services can be costly, limited, or not built for ongoing day-to-day moments.
Project idea

What we propose

A voice-based AI chatbot designed specifically for grief healing. The tool supports gentle conversation, reflection, and storytelling to help children process emotions and share memories in a supportive way.

Examples

Example scenarios

  • Before a parent passes: guided prompts that help a child name feelings and prepare for changes.
  • Family memory sharing: children record favorite stories with a parent (with consent).
  • After loss: the child revisits memories and uses calming activities and reflection prompts.

Discussion prompt for caregivers: “How would you feel if your child used a tool like this?” This helps set boundaries, expectations, and consent.

Ethics & Safety

Safety principles

  • Consent-first: caregivers approve any saved content or voice training.
  • Transparency: the tool clearly states it is AI and not the real person.
  • Not therapy: supports coping skills and reflection; does not replace professional care.
  • Boundaries: avoids medical advice and provides “talk to a trusted adult” guidance.
