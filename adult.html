Adult / Caregiver Overview

This page explains what the grief-support chatbot is, how caregivers would set it up, and the safety boundaries. It is meant to support conversations—not replace therapy or real support.

What the chatbot is

A grief-support chatbot designed for teens (13+) to help with reflection, memory sharing, and coping prompts—especially during a loved one’s serious illness (including hospice) and after loss.

  • Validates feelings without trying to “fix” grief
  • Encourages reflection and journaling-style prompts
  • Suggests outside support when needed
Support tool • Not licensed care

Quick caregiver comfort check

Prompt: “How would you feel if your child used a tool like this?”
Many caregivers prefer trying it themselves first and setting limits.
  • Try it together first
  • Set time limits
  • Decide what topics/phrases to block

How chats are structured (what to expect)

  • Step 1 — Check-in: mood + energy + “how hard is today?”
  • Step 2 — Choice: reflect, remember, or ground (short coping exercise)
  • Step 3 — Prompts: journaling-style questions; user can skip or stay quiet
  • Step 4 — Wrap-up: summarize what helped + suggest a next step (drink water, text someone, take a break)
  • Escalation: if someone seems unsafe or overwhelmed, it encourages reaching a trusted adult/professional
Goal: clear structure + emotional safety + no pressure to talk before they’re ready.

Setup (conceptual)

  • 1) Choose a mode: Teen (13+) language + boundaries
  • 2) Consent controls: caregiver approves features + saved content
  • 3) Customize limits: time limits, blocked topics/phrases, and “always encourage outside support” rules
  • 4) Privacy choices: minimize stored data; allow “no saving” mode

About “voice replication” (we adjusted this)

Based on feedback, we do not frame this as “replicating” a loved one. Instead, the safer concept is Voice Memories: caregiver-approved, pre-recorded clips (e.g., a bedtime message, encouragement, a story).

Why: users worried voice replication could feel too real, create over-reliance, or replace human connection. Voice Memories keeps the comfort while avoiding “this is them” framing.
Voice Memories • optional

Safety, ethics, and limitations

  • Not a professional: clearly states it is not licensed mental health care
  • No harmful advice: avoids pushing disclosure or giving medical guidance
  • Clarity of purpose: grief support + reflection; not “a friend replacement”
  • Privacy-first: minimal data, caregiver controls, and clear explanation of what’s saved
  • Outside support: encourages therapy/real help when grief is severe or risks are present
This is an academic concept landing page and does not provide medical or therapeutic services. If someone feels unsafe or in crisis, contact local emergency services or a trusted professional.
