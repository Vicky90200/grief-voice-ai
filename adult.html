For Adults & Caregivers

This page explains the project concept, who it supports, and the safety/consent principles behind an AI voice chatbot for grief healing for children facing a parent’s serious illness or hospice care.

What the bot is

This project explores an AI voice chatbot for grief healing designed to support children coping with a parent’s serious illness (including hospice) and/or loss. The bot uses conversation prompts to help children name feelings and share memories in a gentle, age-appropriate way.

Support tool • Not therapy

“Replicating voice” (what that means)

With caregiver consent, the system can be configured to speak in a voice similar to a loved one using recorded audio samples. The goal is comfort and memory connection — not pretending the loved one is truly present.

Key boundary: the experience should be clearly framed as AI-generated, not the real person.

How an adult would set it up (concept flow)

  • 1) Choose settings: select “Kids Mode” language level + topics to avoid.
  • 2) Consent + comfort check: caregiver confirms boundaries and decides if voice is enabled.
  • 3) Provide audio (optional): upload short recordings (stories, messages, greetings).
  • 4) Review: caregiver listens to samples and approves before a child can use it.
  • 5) Start using: the child gets guided prompts (feelings + memories), with caregiver oversight.
Caregiver reflection prompt: “How would you feel if your child used a tool like this?” This supports consent, boundaries, and emotional readiness.

What it can do (examples)

  • Memory sharing: prompts like “Tell me a favorite story about your parent.”
  • Emotional check-ins: “What are you feeling today?” with simple coping options.
  • Calming routines: breathing exercises and grounding prompts on hard days.

Safety, ethics, and limitations

  • Consent-first: caregivers control uploads, voice training, and saved content.
  • Transparency: the bot reminds users it is AI and not the real person.
  • Boundaries: no medical advice, diagnosis, or “this is what your parent would say” claims.
  • Emotional safeguards: encourages talking to trusted adults; provides crisis guidance.
  • Privacy: minimize stored data; protect children’s information.
This is an academic concept landing page and does not provide medical or therapeutic services. If someone feels unsafe or in crisis, contact local emergency services or a trusted professional.
