A place to slow down.

This is a concept for a grief-support AI tool. It can help you reflect, name what you’re feeling, and hold onto memories. You can skip anything. Silence is allowed.

Purpose of the bot

This bot is meant to help you process grief in a low-pressure way—by giving you structure when your brain feels messy. It’s not here to “fix you.” It’s here to help you get through moments.

What it can do
• Offer journaling-style prompts
• Help you name feelings
• Support memory sharing
• Suggest small next steps
What it can’t do
• Replace a real person
• Provide therapy/medical advice
• Tell you what someone “would say”
• Make grief go away
If you feel unsafe or like you might hurt yourself, reach out to a trusted adult right away or contact local emergency services.
Support • Not therapy

What it is

A voice-based AI helper that asks gentle questions and offers reflection prompts. It’s not a real person and not a therapist.

Support tool

What you control

  • You can skip questions
  • You can stop anytime
  • You don’t have to share everything
  • If you feel overwhelmed, take a break and reach out to someone you trust
You choose

Reflection prompts (more like journaling)

  • What part of today felt the hardest?
  • What do you wish people understood about your grief?
  • What’s one thing you miss the most—and one thing you still carry?
  • What kind of support would actually help this week?
If a prompt makes you feel worse, skip it. You can come back later—or not at all.

Memory prompts

  • A memory that keeps replaying is…
  • Something small that reminds me of them is…
  • One thing I learned from them is…
  • If I could say one more thing, it would be…
Remembering is allowed

When you don’t know what to do next

Pick one. The goal isn’t “feel better.” It’s just to make the next few minutes more manageable.

Option A — “Say it out loud” (1 sentence)
Finish this: “Right now, the hardest part is…”
That’s it. One sentence counts.
Option B — Text someone (copy/paste)
Hey, I’m having a hard time. I don’t really need advice — can you just stay with me / talk for a bit?
Option C — Ask for space (copy/paste)
I’m overwhelmed right now. I care, but I can’t talk about this today. Can we check in later?
Option D — Save one memory
Save something small: a photo, a note, a voice message, or 3 sentences about a moment you don’t want to forget.
Option E — “What would I tell a friend?”
Imagine your friend is feeling exactly what you feel. Write one kind sentence you’d tell them — then read it back to yourself.
Option F — Get real support
If it feels too heavy: talk to a trusted adult, counselor, or someone who can actually show up for you.
If you feel unsafe or like you might hurt yourself, reach out to a trusted adult right away or contact local emergency services.
